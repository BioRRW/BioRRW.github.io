About me

I am a Bioinformatician and Research Scientist at Colorado State University. My work delves into the complexities of microbial ecosystems, particularly in the realm of metagenomic analysis and bioinformatics.

In my role as the lead developer of the DRAM software and a contributor to the CoSMic Data Science Team, I've focused on creating software pipelines for high-performance computing, aiming to enhance metagenomic assembly and binning.

During my time as a USDA Agricultural Research Service Fellow, I developed Reads2Resistome, a bioinformatics pipeline tailored for the analysis of bacterial DNA data. This tool simplifies de novo bacterial assembly, antimicrobial resistance gene identification, and virulence factor characterization, providing a streamlined approach for handling sequencing data.

As part of the NIH T32 Training Program, I've been involved in practical aspects like extracting DNA and preparing next-generation sequencing libraries for bacterial isolates. My work extends to genetic data analysis, including exploring Mycobacterium tuberculosis through RNA sequencing, reflecting my proficiency in utilizing molecular biology techniques for comprehensive investigations.
